>I've been looking into the legal issues surrounding administering a project that
>would eventually be integrated into a GNU project. Basically, they are a pain in
>the butt. To contribute anything significant to GCC, one has to sign over their
>copyright to the FSF. That means that for this project to be accepted into GCC,
>either you would all have to sign copy rights to me (and I would later sign mine
>over to the FSF), or all of us would have to sign them to the FSF before
>contributing.
>  
>
I have no problem with that.

>Really, though, there is no reason we can't just work on GCC, itself. We have to
>deal with copyright papers, anyway, and if we joined the project would be more
>visible and in the company of many more experts. 
>  
>
I would agree that more people would be able to help us and this way and 
it saves the whole "eventual integration" PITA.

>Therefore, in spite of the work I've put in to BerliOS in the last week, I
